    Government Schemes

    The Government Schemes feature offers farmers details on agricultural initiatives and subsidies provided by governmental bodies. It provides eligibility criteria, application procedures, and benefits, empowering farmers to access financial assistance and improve farming practices for sustainable agriculture and rural development.

    PMKISAN

    Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) is a government initiative in India aimed at providing direct income support to small and marginal farmers. Under this scheme, eligible farmers receive financial assistance of Rs. 6,000 per year, distributed in three equal installments, directly into their bank accounts. The objective is to supplement the financial needs of farmers and ensure their income stability.

    PMFBY

    The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is like a safety net for Indian farmers. It helps protect them if something bad happens to their crops, like floods, pests, or diseases. Farmers pay a little money as insurance, and if their crops get damaged, they get some money back to help them recover. It's a way to make sure farmers don't lose everything if something goes wrong with their crops, which is really important for our food and our Indian farming communities.

    RKVY

    The Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is an Indian agricultural development scheme that incentivizes states to invest more in agriculture and allied sectors for achieving a 4% annual growth rate. It focuses on innovation, technology adoption, and sustainable practices to enhance productivity and improve farmers' livelihoods.

    eNAM

    The National Agriculture Market (eNAM) is an online trading platform in India that integrates agricultural produce markets across the country, creating a unified national market. It facilitates transparent price discovery and competition, enabling farmers to sell their produce directly to buyers from anywhere in the country. eNAM aims to improve farmers' income by reducing intermediaries and ensuring fair prices for their produce.

    PKVY

    The Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) is an Indian government scheme promoting organic farming among small and marginal farmers. It provides financial assistance for organic inputs, capacity building, and certification. PKVY encourages eco-friendly practices and group formation for better resource utilization. Its goal is to enhance soil fertility, reduce chemical usage, and promote sustainable agricultural practices.

    Soil Health Card Scheme

    The Soil Health Card Scheme provides Indian farmers with essential information about their soil's health, including nutrient levels and recommendations for improvement. Through free soil testing every three years, farmers receive personalized advice to enhance soil fertility and crop productivity. The scheme aims to empower farmers with valuable insights for sustainable agricultural practices.

    NFSM

    The National Food Security Mission (NFSM) is an Indian government initiative focused on increasing the production of rice, wheat, and pulses. It aims to improve productivity through technology dissemination and support to farmers in districts with high potential. NFSM plays a vital role in ensuring food security and enhancing farmers' livelihoods.

    PMKSY

    The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) is an Indian government scheme that aims to improve irrigation efficiency in agriculture. It promotes the optimal use of water resources through initiatives like micro-irrigation and watershed development. PMKSY aims to boost crop productivity and enhance farmers' incomes by ensuring better access to water for agricultural purposes.

    Interest Subvention Scheme

    The Interest Subvention Scheme in India offers subsidized interest rates on agricultural loans. It provides a 2% interest subvention on short-term crop loans up to Rs. 3 lakh, encouraging timely repayment. This initiative aims to ease the financial burden on farmers, improve access to credit, and enhance agricultural productivity.

Reduced interest rates on agricultural loans. Additional interest incentive for prompt repayment. Farmers availing crop loans from scheduled commercial banks, regional rural banks, and cooperative banks. Automatic benefit for eligible farmers, applied by lending institutions while disbursing loans.
    Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme Access to credit for farmers Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare Provides farmers with easy access to credit for agricultural and allied activities through a revolving cash credit facility. Flexible credit facility for agriculture and allied activities. Simplified documentation and repayment terms. All farmers, including individuals, joint cultivators, tenant farmers, and sharecroppers. Farmers can apply through designated lending institutions such as commercial banks, regional rural banks, and cooperative banks by submitting required documents such as land records, identity proof, and loan application form.

Support for sustainable natural resource management and capacity building. Farmers, farmer groups, and other stakeholders adopting sustainable agriculture practices. Implementation through State Agriculture Departments. Farmers can contact their respective State Agriculture Departments for participation and assistance.
    PM Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana Pension for small and marginal farmers Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare Provides a monthly pension of Rs. 3,000 to small and marginal farmers after attaining the age of 60 years. Voluntary and contributory pension scheme. Assured monthly pension to beneficiaries. Small and marginal farmers between 18 and 40 years of age. Farmers can apply through Common Service Centers (CSCs) or online through the PM-KISAN portal.
    Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) Promotion of farm mechanization Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare Promotes the use of modern farm machinery and equipment to increase productivity, reduce drudgery, and improve efficiency in agricultural operations. Financial assistance for purchase and hiring of farm machinery. Capacity building and training for farmers. All farmers, farmer groups, and cooperatives. Farmers can apply through designated implementing agencies such as agriculture departments, cooperative societies, or agricultural machinery banks.
    Pradhan Mantri Gram Sinchayee Yojana (PMGSY) Water conservation and management in rural areas Ministry of Rural Development Focuses on providing irrigation facilities and water conservation measures in rural areas to improve agricultural productivity and livelihoods. Construction of farm ponds, dug wells, water harvesting structures, and renovation of traditional water bodies. Empowerment of local communities for sustainable water management. Rural communities, farmers, and agricultural laborers. Implementation through Rural Development Departments. Local communities can participate in planning and execution of projects.
